# 言リーフ — Kotoleaf

**Leaves between words. Until the leaves fall.**

言リーフ (Kotoleaf) is a live bilingual communication tool for English ↔ Japanese meetings — online and in-person. Rather than acting as a translation crutch, Kotoleaf is a language familiar: a spirit companion that interleaves understanding into conversation, teaches as it translates, and gradually fades as both speakers grow.

## Philosophy

Kotoleaf is not a translator. It's a teacher disguised as one.

The tool monitors live bilingual conversation and **interleaves** contextual support only where it's needed — filling comprehension gaps with targeted subtitles, vocabulary hints, and concept clarifications. It adapts to each speaker's known vocabulary and proficiency, so a beginner sees rich support while an advanced speaker sees only the occasional difficult term surfaced.

The goal is its own obsolescence.

### The Leaf Progression 🍃

Inspired by 言の葉 (*Kotonoha* — "leaves of words"), Kotoleaf's assistance follows a natural lifecycle:

- **🌿 Full Leaf** — Dense interleaving. Near-complete translation with vocabulary annotations. For beginners or highly technical discussions.
- **🌱 Veined Leaf** — Structural support. Key terms, idiomatic expressions, and potential misunderstandings are surfaced. Core meaning is left for the listener.
- **🍂 Skeleton Leaf** — Minimal intervention. Only rare vocabulary, cultural nuance, or detected misunderstandings trigger an interleaf.
- **🍁 Fallen Leaf** *(落ち葉)* — The tool goes quiet. You don't need it anymore. Kotoleaf only activates on-demand when you invoke it for a specific phrase or concept.

Each participant's leaf level is independent and adapts over time.

## How It Works

1. **Live speech recognition** captures both sides of the conversation in their original language.
2. **Comprehension modeling** tracks each participant's vocabulary and estimates understanding in real-time.
3. **Misunderstanding detection** identifies moments where meaning is likely to be lost — false cognates, ambiguous grammar, cultural context gaps, or missing vocabulary.
4. **Interleaving** inserts targeted support (subtitles, glosses, concept notes) only at detected gaps, calibrated to each participant's current leaf level.
5. **Vocabulary tracking** records new words each participant successfully comprehends in context, progressively reducing future interventions.

## Name

The name layers meaning across both languages:

| Layer | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| 言 (*koto*) | Word, speech, language |
| リーフ (*riifu*) | Leaf — growth, interleaving, the Kotonoha (言の葉) heritage |
| Interleaf | The bookbinding term for pages inserted between text for annotation — exactly what the tool does |
| 小鳥 (*kotori*) | Hidden inside the name: "little bird" — a familiar that sits on your shoulder and whispers understanding |
